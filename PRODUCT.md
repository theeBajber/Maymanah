# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Students** — seeking Quranic education (Tajweed, Hifdh, Arabic, Tafsir). Varying levels of technical literacy. Global, multi-lingual. Motivated by spiritual growth.

**Teachers** — volunteer instructors offering mentorship. Need scheduling, session management, student tracking tools.

**Administrators** — oversee users, courses, donations. Need oversight and reporting.

**Donors** — supporters funding the free platform. Need clear, trustworthy giving experience.

## Product Purpose

Make high-quality Quranic education accessible to everyone, regardless of location or means. A non-profit platform connecting students with expert volunteer teachers worldwide — personalized learning, live sessions, certification (Ijazah), and community support. Sadaqah Jariyah model.

Success: students progress in their Quranic journey through real human mentorship, not just content consumption.

## Brand Personality

**Islamic, Trustworthy, Ambient**

- Warm without being sentimental. Noble without being stiff.
- The brand carries the dignity of its subject matter — Quranic education — without feeling institutional or cold.
- "Ambient" means the design creates a quiet, supportive atmosphere; it doesn't demand attention but makes the experience feel safe and reverent.
- Modern interface, timeless values. Not "Islamic-themed" (overdone ornaments, green-dome silhouettes) but Islamic in substance: clarity, care, accessibility, beauty as precision.

## Anti-references

- Overly themed "Islamic" design (green domes, heavy arabesques, clip-art mosques) — the faith is in the substance, not the surface.
- SaaS-cream corporate tool aesthetic — this is a spiritual learning environment, not a B2B dashboard.
- Gamified or juvenile education apps — the tone is dignified, not childish, even for young learners.

## Design Principles

1. **Trustworthy by default** — every UI decision must reinforce trust: clear data handling, transparent donations, honest progress metrics. Users must feel the platform is worthy of their Quranic journey.
2. **Ambient spirituality** — the design should quietly evoke the sacred without ornamentalism. Atmosphere through typography, space, light, and rhythm — not through decorative motifs.
3. **Clarity over decoration** — educational tools earn their keep by getting out of the way. Reduce cognitive load. Make the next action obvious. Beautiful is good; clear is better.
4. **Bridge tradition and modern** — the medium is digital, the content is classical. Honor both: load fonts that serve Arabic beautifully, use spacing that feels generous, make the digital feel as considered as a manuscript.
5. **Dignity at every scale** — a student on mobile, a teacher on desktop, an admin reviewing reports: every surface gets the same care. The design never feels like a "mobile version" or an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Semantic HTML, proper ARIA where needed, keyboard navigable, reduced motion support. Arabic and English RTL/LTR handling as a first-class concern.
